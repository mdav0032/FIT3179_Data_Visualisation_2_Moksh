Tourism across the World

​

This visualisation gives an insight into tourism in the world. It first presents how much tourism traffic different continents and countries receive (starting with an overview then diving deeper into arrivals and departures). It then explains how much profit countries actually make from the tourism and what the most common tourist attractions in the world are. It then takes and even deeper look into tourism in specificially Australia, displaying the difference between domestic and international tourism and the reasons why tourists travel. So, to learn more about tourism, keep reading!

Note: The data ranges from 2000 to 2020. All dollar amounts are in USD$.

​

Overview of Tourism in the World

Note: Each colour represents a different continent (stated in the tooltip).

This shows the number of total arrivals or departures to each country, grouped by continent. As the treemap shows, Europe was the most popular tourist destination for arrivals followed by Asia and North America. In terms of departures, Europe again had the highest number of tourists, followed by North America then Asia this time.

​

Deeper Look into Arrivals

Note: Grey represents countries with missing data.

This shows the total number of visitors arriving at each country in the year specified in the slider. The United States of America, China and France generally have the highest number of international visitors. The country to have the overall highest amount of international visitors in a year is France in 2019 when it had over 217 million visitors - which is over 3 times the actual population of France in 2019 (67 million people). So, for any travellers that may be wondering where to travel to, France can be considered one of the best places to travel to.

​

Deeper Look into Departures

Note: To focus on a specific country, select it from the legend or in the chart. The countries displayed are all those that appear once or more in the top 5 rankings between 2000 and 2020.

This shows the top 5 countries with the most departures in each year from 2000 to 2020. Mexico held the record for most international departures between 2000 and 2005, but lost its title to the United States of America who has held the number 1 ranking for most departures ever since, with only Mexico and China (mainlaind) getting somewhat close to that number.

​

Receipts, Expenditure and Profit

Note: Bars are sorted in the order of decreasing total profit. Profit is calculated as receipts (inflow) - expenditure (outflow).

This shows the amount of profit each country (in the selected continent) made from tourism between 2000 and 2020 by displaying the total receipts each country made (inflow of money from tourists spending money in the country) and the total expenditure (outflow of money from tourists spending money in other countries). As seen in the chart, the country that made the highest tourism profit was the United States of America (in North America) that profited $910 billion. On the other hand, the country that made the biggest loss was Germany (in Europe) which lost $836 billion. This is an important statistic to consider when discussing successful tourism, as the ultimate goal of tourism for governments of countries is to make a profit.

​

50 Most Famous Tourist Attractions

Note: The dendrogram here is structured as continent -> country -> attraction. The city is listed in brackets next to the attraction name.

This shows the 50 most commonly visited tourist attractions in the world, clustered by the continent and country which they are in. Over half of these attractions (26 attractions to be exact) are in the United States of America, with equal second most (6 attractions) being in China and France. In Australia, the only attraction to make the list was the Sydney Opera House. The types of attractions that made this list include theme parks, nature and scenery, historical sites, man-made wonders, and more. This gives an insight into which locations tourists should aim to visit when they go travelling, and also gives an insight into all the amazing attractions different countries have to offer.

​

International vs Domestic Tourism in Australia

Note: Each bar represents the number of tourists. Darker and longer bars mean more tourists. This is based on data from 2020.

This shows the number of total domestic and international passengers in and out of airports in Australia, to understand the difference between domestic and international tourism in Australia. The three Australian airports with the most traffic were Sydney followed by Melbourne followed by Brisbane, and this was the case for both domestic and international travellers for both arrivals and departures. A significant difference can actually be seen between the number of domestic tourists and international tourists, as the number of domestic tourists is either close to double or more than double that of international tourists at all airports for both arrivals and departures. In fact, some airports have no international arrivals or departures. This indicates that Australian tourists prefer travelling to other places within Australia rather than travelling internationally.

​

Purpose of Visits to Australia

Note: This is based on data for the financial year from 2020 to 2021.

This shows the reasons why different tourists came to visit Australia, and the percentage in each category. By an extremely significant margin, the most popular reason for which tourists travelled to Australia was to visit friends and relatives, displaying that Australia is seen more as a place to travel for relationships and spending quality time with people rather than a place specialising in having many physical attractions. Other somewhat common purposes included business reasons, employment reasons, or holiday reasons.
